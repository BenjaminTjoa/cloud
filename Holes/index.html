1. Make a drawing of Camp Green Lake.
Make a nice drawing here.
2. What is the climate like in Camp Green Lake?
It is extremely hot and dry, with no rain for over a hundred years. The lake has dried up, leaving behind a barren wasteland.
3. What’s dangerous about a yellow-spotted lizard?
Its bite is deadly, and there is no known cure for its venom.
4. What kind of place is Camp Green Lake exactly?
It is a juvenile detention center where boys are forced to dig holes in the desert as a form of punishment and character-building.
5. What do you know about Stanley’s parents?
They are poor and struggling financially. Stanley’s father is an inventor, trying to develop a cure for foot odor.
6. Describe Stanley in detail.
Stanley is overweight at the start of the novel, with little self-confidence. He is kind-hearted, determined, and grows stronger, both physically and mentally, throughout the story.
7. What do you know about the first Stanley Yelnats?
Positive: He was ambitious and hardworking. Negative: He was unlucky and got robbed by Kissin’ Kate Barlow.
8. What is special about the name Stanley Yelnats?
It is a palindrome; it reads the same forwards and backwards.
9. What do the boys have to do at Camp Green Lake and why?
They have to dig a hole every day, supposedly to build character, but in reality, the Warden is searching for hidden treasure.
10. Why is there no fence around the camp?
Because the desert is so harsh that escape is nearly impossible.
11. Why do the guards at Camp Green Lake have guns?
To protect themselves from the dangerous wildlife, including yellow-spotted lizards.
12. What is the one rule at camp?
Don't upset the Warden.
13. Why does Mr. Pendanski call the boys by their real names?
To remind them that they are more than just criminals and to encourage self-improvement.
14. Describe the incident that led to Stanley’s arrest in detail.
Stanley was accused of stealing Clyde Livingston’s sneakers, which had fallen from an overpass and landed near him. He was innocent, but the court did not believe him.
15. Why do you think the boys have nicknames for each other?
It builds camaraderie and gives them a sense of identity and belonging.
16. Fill in the following table:
Real name Nickname Why?
Stanley Caveman He becomes stronger and tougher.
Rex X-Ray It’s Rex in pig-Latin.
Hector Zero People think he is dumb.
Mr. Pendanski Mom He acts like a parental figure.

17. What makes digging holes so hard?
The ground is dry and rock-hard, and the heat is exhausting.
18. What’s the rule when they find something?
They must report it to the Warden.
19. Why does Stanley’s overweight help him?
At first, it makes digging harder, but later he becomes stronger and fitter.
20. Why does Madame Zeroni think it’s foolish that Elya wants to marry Myra?
Because Myra is shallow and does not truly love Elya.
21. What does Elya have to do?
Carry a pig up a mountain every day and let it drink from a special stream.
22. Why does Elya change his mind about Myra?
She cannot even choose between him and another man, showing she has no real feelings for him.
23. What promise to Madame Zeroni hadn’t he fulfilled?
He never carried her up the mountain.
24. Why does Stanley blame everything on his dirty-rotten-pig-stealing great-great-grandfather?
Because of the curse placed on the Yelnats family.
25. How does Stanley feel about his first hole?
Exhausted, sore, and miserable.
26. Why does he refuse the ride back to camp?
He wants to prove he can walk back on his own.
27. Explain the wordplay in “wreck room.”
It sounds like “rec room,” but it is actually a wreck.
28. Is Stanley really making friends?
Not at first, but he eventually forms a bond with Zero.
29. Why is the second hole harder?
His muscles are already sore and weak.
30. What does Stanley find?
A gold tube with initials KB.
31. What does X-Ray convince Stanley to do?
To give him anything valuable he finds so he can get a day off.
32. Why does Stanley agree?
He wants to be accepted by the group.
33. Why does the Warden get excited?
Because X-Ray pretends to have found something important.
34. What does the Warden do to Mr. Pendanski?
She slaps him for questioning her authority.
35. What does the Warden do to Zero?
She humiliates him and implies he is useless.
36. Why does Stanley start to teach Zero to read?
Zero wants to learn, and Stanley feels guilty for treating him unfairly.
37. How does the Warden punish the boys?
She makes them dig in the same spot over and over again.
38. Why does Zero run away?
He is tired of being mistreated and insulted.
39. Why does Stanley follow him?
He feels responsible and wants to help.
40. What is “God’s Thumb”?
A mountain shaped like a thumb, where Stanley’s ancestor survived.
41. How do Stanley and Zero survive?
They find onions and water on the mountain.
42. What does Zero reveal?
He was the one who stole Clyde Livingston’s sneakers.
43. What effect does this have on Stanley?
He realizes his bad luck was actually destiny.
44. What is buried in the hole they return to?
A suitcase with Stanley’s name on it.
45. Why does the Warden want it?
It contains valuable treasure.
46. Why can’t she take it?
Stanley’s lawyer arrives just in time.
47. What happens to the camp?
It is shut down.
48. What happens to Zero?
He finds his mother and starts a new life.
49. What happens to Stanley?
His family becomes rich, and his bad luck is lifted.
50. What lesson does Stanley learn?
That persistence and kindness can break any curse.
51. Why do the yellow-spotted lizards not bite Stanley and Zero?
Because they have been eating onions, which repel the lizards.
52. What does the suitcase contain?
Jewelry, stocks, and valuable documents.
53. What does Stanley’s father invent?
A cure for foot odor.
54. What happens to the Warden?
She loses everything and is forced to leave Camp Green Lake.
55. What is ironic about Stanley’s luck?
He thought he was unlucky, but everything led to a positive outcome.
56. Why does the lake fill with water again?
The curse is broken, and rain finally returns.
57. What does Zero finally get?
A proper education and a home.
58. What does Stanley learn about his family?
That fate and family history are deeply connected.
59. What is the message of the book?
That hard work, friendship, and believing in yourself can change your destiny.
60. What is the final scene of the book?
Stanley’s family celebrating their fortune, while Zero reunites with his mother.
